About

Hello! I am Joey, or officially Chiao-Yin Hsiao / 蕭喬尹. I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the School of Information, University of Michigan (UMSI). I am now a member of the Social Innovation Group (SIG), advised by Prof. Tawanna Dillahunt. My research interests are Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), and Social Computing. I am currently exploring how location-based social-matching applications, i.e., people-nearby applications, can support newcomers such as newly arrived immigrants.

I received a bachelor's degree and a master's degree from Dept. of Computer Science and Information Engineering at National Taiwan University (NTU-CSIE), advised by Prof. Yung-Jen Hsu. Before starting my Ph.D., I worked as a research assistant in Intel-NTU Context Computing Center with Prof. Hao-Chuan Wang and Prof. Bing-Yu Chen.

I believe in the theory - strength of the weak ties, so I love to meet new friends and introduce a friend to another friend. I also love collecting hats. Let me know if you know where to find cool hats!

For more details, please check my CV.
